Aluminium Wire Rods

Aluminium wire rods are long, cylindrical metal rods primarily made from Aluminium. They are used as raw materials for producing Aluminium wires and are widely employed in various industries due to their lightweight, excellent conductivity, and corrosion resistance.

Features

Lightweight

Aluminium wire rods are significantly lighter than their counterparts made from other materials like copper or steel

High Conductivity

Aluminium wire rods exhibit excellent electrical conductivity.

Corrosion Resistance

Aluminium naturally forms a protective oxide layer which protects it from corrosion.

Ductility

Aluminium is a highly ductile metal which makes it very easy to work with.

Thermal Resistance

Aluminium possesses good thermal conductivity which means it can easily dissipate heat.

Eco-Friendly

Aluminium is a highly recyclable metal.

Applications

Aluminum wire rods are the primary raw material for manufacturing overhead transmission lines. They are drawn into wires and cables of various sizes and configurations to carry electrical power over long distances.

Overhead Transmission Lines

Aluminum wire rods are used to manufacture windings in transformers, which are essential components for stepping up or stepping down voltage in power systems.

Transformer Windings

Aluminum wire rods are used to produce bus bars, which are conductive bars used to distribute electrical power within substations and switchyards.

Busbars

Aluminum wire rods are used to produce surge arresters, which are devices that protect electrical equipment from voltage surges caused by lightning strikes or switching operations.

Surge Arresters

Aluminum wire rods are used to manufacture HVDC cables, which are used to transmit large amounts of electrical power over long distances with minimal energy loss.

High-Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) Cables
